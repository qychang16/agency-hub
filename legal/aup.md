# Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective: 5 May 2026**

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") governs your use of the Tel-Cloud platform and related services (the "Service"), provided by Y.E.C Consultancy (UEN 202231751D), trading as Tel-Cloud. The AUP is incorporated by reference into our Terms of Service. Capitalised terms used but not defined here have the meanings given in the Terms of Service.

By using the Service, you agree to comply with this AUP and to ensure that your Authorised Users do the same.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this AUP is to protect Tel-Cloud, our Customers, Recipients of messages sent through the Service, and the third-party platforms we integrate with, from abuse, harm, and legal risk. This AUP is not exhaustive; conduct that violates the spirit of this AUP may also be grounds for action even if not specifically listed.

## 2. Prohibited Content

You must not use the Service to send, store, or transmit content that:

- Is unlawful, fraudulent, defamatory, obscene, or otherwise objectionable;
- Infringes intellectual property, privacy, publicity, or other rights of any third party;
- Promotes hatred, harassment, or violence against any person or group;
- Constitutes child sexual abuse material or sexually explicit content involving minors;
- Promotes or facilitates the sale of regulated goods or services without proper authorisation, including narcotics, firearms, prescription drugs, or unlicensed financial products;
- Contains malware, ransomware, viruses, or other malicious code;
- Discloses another person's confidential or personal data without lawful basis; or
- Misrepresents the sender's identity or impersonates any person or organisation.

## 3. Prohibited Conduct

You must not:

- Send unsolicited bulk or commercial messages ("spam") through the Service;
- Send messages to Recipients who have not provided valid consent where consent is required under applicable law or platform policy;
- Continue to send messages to Recipients who have opted out, requested cessation, or otherwise withdrawn consent;
- Bypass, disable, or interfere with security or authentication features of the Service;
- Reverse engineer, decompile, or attempt to extract source code from the Service, except to the extent permitted by applicable law;
- Use automated means to access the Service in a manner that imposes an unreasonable load, including scraping, crawling, or denial-of-service attacks;
- Probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the Service or any related system without our prior written consent;
- Use the Service to develop a competing product or service, or to benchmark the Service for publication without our prior written consent;
- Resell, sublicense, or otherwise commercially exploit the Service except as expressly permitted by your subscription;
- Share login credentials with unauthorised individuals;
- Use the Service in any manner that violates applicable law, including export controls, sanctions, anti-spam, anti-fraud, and consumer protection laws.

## 4. Messaging-Specific Requirements

In addition to the rules above, the following apply when sending messages through the Service:

### 4.1 Consent

You must obtain and maintain records of valid consent from Recipients before sending non-transactional messages where consent is required under applicable law or the policies of the relevant messaging platform. You must be able to produce evidence of consent on request.

### 4.2 Opt-out

All non-transactional messaging campaigns must include a clear and easy method for Recipients to opt out. You must promptly honour opt-out requests, suppress opted-out contacts from future campaigns, and not penalise Recipients for opting out.

### 4.3 Sender identification

Messages must clearly identify the sender. You must not disguise the origin of a message or use deceptive subject lines, sender names, or display names.

### 4.4 Compliance with third-party platform policies

Messages sent through third-party platforms, including but not limited to Meta's WhatsApp Business Platform, must comply with that platform's commerce, business, messaging, and template policies. You acknowledge that violation of platform policies may result in suspension of your access to that platform, independently of any action by Tel-Cloud.

### 4.5 Sensitive content categories

Use of the Service for messaging in regulated or sensitive industries, such as financial services, healthcare, gambling, dating, or political campaigning, must comply with all applicable laws and platform-specific policies. We may require additional information or impose additional conditions before enabling such use.

## 5. Reporting and Enforcement

### 5.1 Reporting violations

To report a suspected violation of this AUP, contact legal@tel-cloud.sg. We investigate credible reports promptly.

### 5.2 Investigation

We may investigate suspected violations, including by reviewing account activity, message content, and metadata, in accordance with our Privacy Policy and applicable law. You agree to cooperate reasonably with any such investigation.

### 5.3 Action we may take

Where we determine that a violation has occurred, or where the violation is sufficiently serious or urgent, we may take any of the following actions, in our sole discretion and without liability:

- Issue a warning;
- Require remediation within a stated timeframe;
- Throttle, restrict, or remove specific features for your account;
- Suspend the account in whole or in part;
- Terminate the account and the underlying subscription;
- Notify the relevant third-party platform, regulator, or law enforcement authority where required or appropriate; or
- Disclose information to the extent required by law.

Where reasonable and appropriate, we will provide notice and an opportunity to cure before taking action. We are not required to do so where the violation poses an immediate risk to Tel-Cloud, our Customers, Recipients, or third-party platforms.
