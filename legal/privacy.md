# Privacy Policy

**Effective: 5 May 2026**

This Privacy Policy explains how Y.E.C Consultancy (UEN 202231751D), trading as Tel-Cloud ("Tel-Cloud", "we", "us", "our"), collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data when you access or use our software-as-a-service platform, websites, and related services (collectively, the "Service").

We are committed to handling your personal data in accordance with the Singapore Personal Data Protection Act 2012 ("PDPA") and, where applicable, the EU General Data Protection Regulation ("GDPR") and other relevant data protection laws.

By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy.

## 1. Who this Policy Applies To

This Policy applies to:

- Customers and account holders who subscribe to the Service ("Customers");
- End users authorised by a Customer to access the Service ("Authorised Users");
- Recipients of communications sent through the Service ("Recipients"); and
- Visitors to our websites and prospective customers.

Where Tel-Cloud processes personal data on behalf of a Customer (for example, contact records or candidate information uploaded into the platform), the Customer is the data controller and Tel-Cloud acts as the data processor. The Customer's own privacy notice governs the relationship between the Customer and the data subject.

## 2. Personal Data We Collect

### 2.1 Information you provide

- Account information: name, email address, phone number, organisation name, billing details, role, and login credentials.
- Content you upload: contact lists, candidate records, message templates, attachments, and any other data you submit to the Service.
- Support correspondence: information you provide when you contact us for support, sales, or feedback.

### 2.2 Information collected automatically

- Usage data: pages visited, features used, timestamps, referring URLs, and actions taken within the Service.
- Device and log data: IP address, browser type, operating system, device identifiers, and crash reports.
- Cookies and similar technologies: used for authentication, session management, security, analytics, and to remember your preferences.

### 2.3 Information from third parties

- Authentication providers (such as Google) when you sign in using single sign-on.
- Payment processors that confirm transactions on our behalf.
- Third-party messaging platforms, including but not limited to Meta's WhatsApp Business Platform, that return delivery status, message events, and recipient identifiers.

## 3. How We Use Personal Data

We use personal data to:

- Provide, operate, maintain, and improve the Service;
- Authenticate users and secure accounts;
- Process transactions and send service-related communications;
- Deliver messages on behalf of Customers through third-party messaging platforms;
- Provide customer support and respond to enquiries;
- Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents;
- Comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements; and
- With your consent, send marketing communications about our products. You may opt out at any time.

## 4. Legal Bases for Processing

Where the GDPR or comparable laws apply, we rely on the following legal bases:

- Performance of a contract: to provide the Service you have subscribed to.
- Legitimate interests: to secure, improve, and market our Service, balanced against your rights.
- Legal obligation: to comply with applicable laws and lawful requests.
- Consent: where required, for example for certain marketing or cookies.

## 5. How We Share Personal Data

We do not sell personal data. We share personal data only as follows:

### 5.1 Service providers

We share data with vetted third-party service providers who help us operate the Service, including cloud hosting (such as Railway and similar infrastructure providers), email delivery, analytics, and customer support tooling. These providers are bound by contractual obligations to protect your data.

### 5.2 Messaging platforms

When you send messages through the Service, we transmit the message content and Recipient identifiers to the relevant third-party messaging platform (including but not limited to Meta's WhatsApp Business Platform) so that the message can be delivered. Such platforms process the data in accordance with their own terms and privacy policies.

### 5.3 Legal and safety disclosures

We may disclose personal data when required by law, court order, or government request, or where we reasonably believe disclosure is necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Tel-Cloud, our Customers, or others.

### 5.4 Business transfers

If Tel-Cloud is involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, personal data may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to standard confidentiality protections.

## 6. International Data Transfers

Tel-Cloud is operated from Singapore, and your personal data may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in countries outside your country of residence. Where required, we put appropriate safeguards in place, such as Standard Contractual Clauses, to ensure that transferred data receives an adequate level of protection.

## 7. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to provide the Service, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Customer-uploaded content is retained for the duration of the Customer's subscription and for a reasonable wind-down period thereafter, unless the Customer requests earlier deletion. Specific retention periods are documented in our internal data retention schedule, available on request.

## 8. Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Measures include encryption in transit, role-based access control, audit logging, and regular review of our security practices. No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 9. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to:

- Access the personal data we hold about you;
- Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data;
- Request deletion of your personal data;
- Object to or restrict certain processing;
- Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent;
- Request portability of your data; and
- Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority, including the Personal Data Protection Commission of Singapore.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at legal@tel-cloud.sg. We will respond within the timeframes required by applicable law.

## 10. Children's Privacy

The Service is intended for business use and is not directed to individuals under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided personal data to us, please contact us and we will take appropriate steps to delete it.

## 11. Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to operate and improve the Service. You can control cookies through your browser settings. Disabling certain cookies may affect functionality such as staying signed in.

## 12. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated through the Service or by email. The "Effective" date at the top of this Policy indicates when it was last revised. Your continued use of the Service after changes take effect constitutes acceptance of the updated Policy.
